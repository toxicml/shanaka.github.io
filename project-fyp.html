Autonomous Mobile Robotic Manipulator

For Smart Agriculture in Sri Lankan Polytunnels

Overview

This project focuses on the Design and Development of a Multi-functional Autonomous Mobile Robotic Manipulator for Smart Agriculture. The goal was to address challenges in Sri Lanka such as labor scarcity, inefficient disease management, and limited access to modern technology. The robot targets a high-value crop, Muriya F1 Capsicum, cultivated in polytunnels.

The system combines three modules. A mobile ground platform enables autonomous navigation across uneven terrain. A 3-DoF Cartesian manipulator provides vertical and horizontal reach to work within the canopy. A 2-DoF gimbal-mounted end effector handles precise tasks such as targeted spraying and localized inspection.

Capabilities include disease and stress detection using RGB and NIR imaging with vegetation indices, selective treatment via a precision sprayer to reduce chemical use, and simple intra-field transport tasks. The prototype was validated in simulation and physical tests, showing coordinated navigation and actuation and providing a scalable base for future multi-crop extensions.

Highlights

  • Operates under plant canopies in narrow polytunnel rows with coordinated base, arm and gimbal motion
  • Provides row level navigation with waypointing and repeat routines for reliable coverage
  • Adapts speed with pause, resume and safe stop features
  • Uses RGB and NIR vision with NDVI and machine learning for plant stress and disease detection
  • Combines detection and spraying within each scan cycle for efficient treatment
  • Dual nozzle gimbal delivers adjustable cone sprays and upward jets for full leaf coverage including undersides
  • Runs on a battery powered system with BMS and fused distribution monitored in the operator GUI
  • Operator dashboard provides telemetry, alerts and spray event logging
  • Modular design allows extensions such as towing and data management
  • Validated through simulation and a full scale prototype
